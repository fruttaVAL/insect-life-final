About Bedbugs

Bedbugs are small, reddish-brown parasitic insects that feed on the blood of humans and animals. They are often found in beds and furniture, but they can inhabit any areas that are small and cozy such as in between wood floors, vents, or even outlets. They are spread through contact and can latch onto items and luggage that a person is carrying which will lead their way into their home to feed on their host(s). As long as there is a host for bedbugs to feed on, they will continue for as long as they live.

My Personal Experience

When my house became victim to the bedbugs in 2021, it was a horrifying experience. At first, I was not sure how to diagnose the numerous amounts of bug bites that I was getting overnight. I knew about bedbugs but I did not know how sneaky they were and they continued to feed off my skin every night. It got really bad to the point where I got insomnia due to the fear of getting that itchy feeling of a bug bite. Even if I changed rooms, it was only a temporary solution as I would wake up with more bites in a few days without knowing how.

Bedbugs are very sneaky and I learned that the hard way. I never saw one for awhile and neither did anyone in my family. My brother said my itchiness was coming from an allergic reaction to the detergent and that we should change it. Until one night, I woke up to one of the little guys crawling up into my sleeve filled up with blood. Not a great sight. The smart thing to do would've been to call an exterminator right away to get rid of them. However for me, I resorted to the internet and researched what I could do in order to get rid of bedbugs at home. I vacuumed everywhere in each room and found hidden bugs laying underneath in tight areas of the bed frames, and then I set bedbug traps under my bed posts to catch them if they tried crawling onto my bed which worked for the most part. But they kept on coming back so we ended up calling the exterminator anyway. Nonetheless, my peace finally came back and I am hoping to spread some things that I know about these pests that I accrued through first hand experience.

Identification

Bedbugs have six legs with a pair of antennae and are brown or reddish depending on if they have eaten recently or not. They are about the size of an appleseed. The images at the top of the website should give you an indicator of what these bugs look like. The characteristic that will set them apart from other bugs with similar physical characteristics such as fleas is their behavior. Bedbugs are not erratic and move relatively slow. They do not fly but rather crawl their way onto a location.

Signs

There are many signs of possible bedbugs in your house but they are subtle. Take note of these signs if you suspect that you have bedbugs in your house.

Finally if you have a group of bug bites in one area especially in areas that make contact with your bed, they are most likely the result of bedbugs.

Prevention

Follow these tips to prevent bedbug infestations:

  • Regularly inspect and clean your living space. Bedbugs like to take shelter wherever they can and will not hesitate to hide in your laundry.
  • Use mattress covers.
  • Be cautious when traveling to avoid bringing bedbugs home. Make sure to clean your clothes directly after a vacation and keep your bags in the garage.

Treatment

If you suspect a bedbug infestation, consider the following treatments:

  • Hire a professional pest control service.
  • Wash and vacuum affected areas thoroughly.
  • Dispose of infested items and clothing properly.

Some things NOT to do include:

  • Move items around from infested areas. It is difficult to determine if items in your room are also inhabited with bugs so this will spread the issue.
  • Sleep in a different room. Bedbugs can actually migrate at a relatively quick speed to different rooms if they have no food, which will only lead to spreading the problem unless you can stop them from crawling up to your sleep area.
  • Bedbug bombs. This is a temporary solution which only displaces the bugs.

Conclusion

One thing I learned from bedbugs is that they are extremely intelligent. They will go at lengths to hide themselves from humans. They are able to detect the changes in CO2 levels when a human sleeps, so essentially they will only come out when you are asleep. Their hiding spots are very well hidden and it is best to check in your bedframe and really tear apart every corner of your room to find them. If you do not do that, you will just go through the same suffering like I did and go through a stage of denial. Perhaps its not bugs and its your imagination. If you are facing a problem with bedbugs, take them serious. Do not underestimate their will to live through everything and annoy you constantly.
